Look, I'm gonna say it straight: these regs are broken. Not "teething problems" broken, not "give it time" broken—actually broken. And the worst part? F1 went full electric when it should've just... not done that.

## The Problem: 50/50 Doesn't Work

Here's my thing: F1 needs to be about the engine. That's what makes it racing. But instead we've got this half-and-half hybrid split that's turned the cars into glorified mobile batteries. Maximum 20% electric—that's it. That's the sweet spot. Just enough to smoothen out the torque curve on the engine, make the power delivery usable, and keep the cars actually performant.

Right now? Lap times are looking like F2. F2! We're literally slower than the feeder series looked a few years ago. That's not progress, that's a step backwards dressed up as "sustainability."

## The Overtaking Nightmare

And don't get me started on the overtakes. Yo-yo overtaking—that's what we've got now. Someone gets a tow, flies past, then gets passed three corners later. It's not racing, it's just trading positions. There's no actual pace difference, no genuine performance advantage.

Instead of drivers pushing to the maximum and extracting every bit of performance from their car, they're tiptoeing around energy management. Super clipping, backing off, nursing their battery. That's not what racing should be about—it should be about driver skill and balls-out performance, not who's managed their battery better.

There have been some cool battles, I'll give it that. But they feel accidental—like, good racing happens despite the regs, not because of them. That shouldn't be how it works.

## The Safety Issues

Here's what really gets me though—the dangerous stuff. We've had instances where cars have nearly smashed into the back of each other because of the energy systems being unpredictable. That's not acceptable. Drivers shouldn't be pulling up because the hybrid system's done something weird and they've lost all power mid-braking zone. That's a recipe for a proper accident.

## But Here's the Thing...

I'm not completely doom-mongering. Yeah, the regs are a mess right now, but it's also fair to say this is normal when you make massive changes. Teams are still figuring it out, the power units are basically prototypes, and there's stuff getting ironed out every weekend. That's fine—that's expected.

We're moving in the right direction on some fronts. The competitiveness is there, the unpredictability's actually interesting in parts, and the technical challenge is real. So I'm not saying "scrap everything"—I'm saying "fix it."

## What Needs to Happen

FIA needs to seriously reconsider the 50/50 split. Go back to favouring the engine. Make the hybrid system a tool for the engine rather than half the power unit. Get the lap times back to where they should be. Sort out the safety issues with sudden power loss. That's it.

If they do that? You'll actually have racing again instead of this energy management lottery. The cars will be faster, the overtakes will mean something, and drivers won't be worried about their hybrid system cutting out mid-corner.

The regulations have potential, but they're fundamentally wrong right now. And I think F1 needs to bite the bullet and admit that before we get another full season of this.
